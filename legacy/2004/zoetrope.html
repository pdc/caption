La forêt des zoetropes

This year 2004, on the fine summer's weekend of the 14-15 August, we may look forward to the 12th appearance of Caption, the United Kingdom's longest-running Alternative and Independent Comics Convention, this year taking place in the new venue of Wolfson College, Linton St., Oxford. In this location of glorious, nay, shocking modernity there will be on display a wonder of the mechanickal age; a veritable forest of extraordinary comics zoetropes.

What is this “ zoetrope ” of which you speak?

A zoetrope is a spinning apparatus through which static pictures may be given the appearance of movement. Put simply, a sequence of twelve images drawn to follow on from each other are moved in front of the eye at speed, and through the persistence of vision, become a short moving image, or “ movie ” as the popular taste has it. Caption is eager to obtain pictures from a great number of talented individuals, and this sheet is your invitation to participate.

Very well, what must I do?

To place your own tree within the zoetrope forest, you must send, to the address below, no fewer than four and no more than twelve images of dimensions no smaller than 1cm and no greater than 3cm in either direction. All images must be of the same size and follow on from each other, to create the illusion of movement. Images may be submitted as paper artwork, concept sketches, computer images or animated gifs.

To whom should I send my art?

The organiser of the zoetrope forest is Jeremy Dennis. You may send items for the exhibition (or correspondence) to her at the Caption address, which is 18 Hawkins Street, Oxford, OX4 1YD. Alternatively you may send her items via email to cleanskies@yahoo.co.uk, or converse with her online at cleanskies.livejournal.com.
