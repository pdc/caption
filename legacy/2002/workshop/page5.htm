If you do it right, they should end up not needing you. And if I occasionally use that excuse to down a few too many in the bar while I'm giving the teams their space, well it's only natural. I have a hangover and therefore must drink. They have magic markers, and therefore must draw.

By the time I got upstairs again, the cops had boiled their story down to a single, terrifyingly concentrated page, the detectives had welded their group into a production-line comics team (pose, sketch, pencil, ink, finish) and the private dicks had disappeared entirely. I found them outside, stood under a lamp-post, three pages drawn and three more planned, their differences resolved by changing artists before each new scene.


There were still problems to be solved, of course. A couple of story elements that just wouldn't stick. A wig that didn't exactly say "Dame". A small matter of two of the pieces being kidnapped so some crazy dame could hold up a comics stall. But that's why I'm there, to sort out all those little problems. To clear the way, so people can get on without getting hung up on egos and anxieties and panic about this not being good enough or that not being just right and all the usual crap that holds a good story down.

And to hold the spare key for the handcuffs.
